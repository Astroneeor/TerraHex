Throughout history, location and tracking has been a key part of both the world, and our day-to-day lives. From compassess to maps, we have made many advancements in the fields of naviagtion, locating, and travelling. We began by gazing out at the heavens, wondering if we could reach them, and grew to navigate the murky seas that trapped our ancestors on the isolated land-masses, to traverse the vast canyons and plains that covered the wilderness, and the unexplored land we now inhabit. Now, as we once more set our sights towards the skies, and the promise of life among the stars, we have become masters of our little blue planet, surrounding it in sensors, and hunks of metal that orbit at 28 800 km/hr, to provide us with the information we yearned for so long ago, the secrets to navigation.

We have now even gotten to a point, that navigation and the pieces of metal allowing us to use it, is even accessible to those who barely understand them, first year students of engineering of all things.
